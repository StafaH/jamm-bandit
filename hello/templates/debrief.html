Debrief Form:

Thank you so much for participating in today’s study! We really appreciate you taking the time to contribute to our scientific understanding of the way that we perceive and think about people.
About the Study
Background: Previous research has shown that people quickly judge others based on nonverbal information, such as the face. Here, we showed you pictures of men’s face and wanted to see what about their face would influence your judgments of them. For instance, some participants were asked how attractive the men looked and other participants were asked how happy or sad the faces looked. We will see if these and other ratings of the faces relate to each other.
More information: To learn more, see : Ambady, N., Bernieri, F.J., & Richeson, J.A. (2000). Towards a histology of social behavior: Judgmental accuracy from thin slices of the behavioral stream. Advances in Experimental Social Psychology, 32, 201-271.

If you do decide to withdraw, you will still receive compensation for your time and will not be penalized or punished in any way. You don’t even have to say why, and we promise not to pry.
